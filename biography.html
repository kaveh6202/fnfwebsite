My Journey

From Student to Teacher

A path of continuous learning and growth.

2018

First Trip to Rishikesh

My transformative journey began with a pilgrimage to Rishikesh, the yoga capital of the world. Standing by the sacred Ganges River, surrounded by the Himalayan foothills, I felt a profound calling to deepen my practice and understanding of yoga's ancient wisdom.

2018

200hr Ashtanga Teacher Training

Immersed myself in the disciplined practice of Ashtanga yoga in Rishikesh. This intensive training taught me the importance of breath, alignment, and the meditative flow of traditional sequences. Here I learned that yoga is not just physical practice, but a complete system for transformation.

2018

200hr Graduation

A moment of profound accomplishment and new beginnings. Graduating from my first teacher training in Rishikesh marked not just the completion of intensive study, but the birth of my calling as a yoga teacher. I carried with me the sacred teachings and the confidence to share this ancient practice.

2019

Second Trip to India - Kerala

Returned to India with deeper purpose, this time exploring the lush landscapes of Kerala. The serene backwaters and tropical beauty provided a different but equally profound setting for advancing my yoga studies. Kerala's Ayurvedic traditions enriched my understanding of holistic wellness.

2019

300hr Ashtanga & Hatha Graduation

Achieved my advanced 300-hour certification in both Ashtanga and Hatha yoga in Kerala. This comprehensive training deepened my understanding of yoga philosophy, anatomy, and the subtle differences between dynamic and gentle practices. I emerged as a well-rounded teacher, ready to guide students of all levels on their wellness journeys.

Teaching Philosophy

"My teaching philosophy is rooted in respect for each student's journey. I believe classes should be structured by expertise so that everyone practices in a space suited to their level, free from comparison or pressure.

Adjustment is central to my approach, as yoga should adapt to the individual body rather than forcing the body into an ideal. I emphasize that yoga is not about constantly pushing limits but about showing up with mindfulness—because as long as you attend with presence, yoga is already working.

Through awareness in posture and presence on the mat, we nurture clarity, patience, and balance, which naturally extend into joy and a deeper sense of life beyond the practice."

— My Approach to Teaching
